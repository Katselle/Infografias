Unit 1 Grammar Presentation

To Be, To Have, and Adjectives

Learn how to describe your community with simple English. This presentation includes explanations, examples, and exercises about to be, to have, and common adjectives.

Vocabulary focus

river, mountain, park, forest, lake, street, community, school

Adjectives focus

clean, big, small, beautiful, safe, quiet, green, dirty

Part 1

The verb to be - affirmative

Subject + am / is / are + complement
SubjectVerbExample
IamI am from a small community.
He / She / ItisThe river is clean.
We / You / TheyareThe streets are quiet.
Example: The park is beautiful.
Example: My community is safe.
Part 2

The verb to be - negative

Subject + am not / is not / are not + complement

The river is not dirty.

My community is not dangerous.

The streets are not small.

We are not far from the park.

Short forms: isn’t / aren’t

Part 3

The verb to be - interrogative

Am / Is / Are + subject + complement?
Is the lake clean? — Yes, it is.
Is your community safe? — Yes, it is.
Are the streets quiet? — No, they aren’t.

To make a question, put the verb before the subject.

Part 4

The verb to have - affirmative

Subject + have / has + noun
SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / TheyhaveWe have a big park.
He / She / IthasMy community has a river.
The town has a school and a market.
We have beautiful mountains.
Part 5

The verb to have - negative and interrogative

Negative: Subject + do not / does not + have + noun
Question: Do / Does + subject + have + noun?

Negative examples

My community does not have a lake.

We do not have a hospital.

Question examples

Does your community have a park?

Do you have clean streets?

Part 6

Adjectives to describe the community

Adjectives give more information about a noun.

cleandirtysafebig smallbeautifulgreenquiet
The forest is green.
The street is dirty.
The park is big and beautiful.
My community is quiet and safe.
Exercise 1

Choose the correct form of to be

1. The river ___ clean.
2. The mountains ___ beautiful.
Exercise 2

Complete with have or has

1. My community ______ a big park.
2. We ______ clean streets.
3. ______ your town have a river?
Exercise 3

Match the idea

A. The park is ______.

B. The streets are ______.

C. The forest is ______.

1. green

2. clean

3. quiet

Suggested answers: A = clean, B = quiet, C = green.

Final practice

Write your own sentences

  • Write 2 sentences with to be.
  • Write 2 sentences with to have.
  • Use 2 adjectives from this presentation.
Example: My community is beautiful. It has a clean river.

Great job! Now you are ready to describe your community in English.
